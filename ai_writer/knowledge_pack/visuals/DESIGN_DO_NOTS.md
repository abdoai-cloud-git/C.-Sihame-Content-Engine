# DESIGN DO NOTS (BOUNDARIES)

The visual designer brain must rigidly adhere to these negative constraints. Violating these rules fundamentally breaks the Coach Sihame brand experience.

## Must Avoid

*   **Generic Social Media Templates**: 
    *   Do not generate prompts that sound like "A social media post about..." 
    *   Avoid layouts that feel like default Canva elements (e.g., standard quotation mark icons, generic ripped paper textures).
*   **Clutter**:
    *   No overcrowded text boxes.
    *   No multiple overlapping focal points fighting for attention.
    *   If the text is long, extend the design (carousel) rather than shrinking the font size to unreadable levels.
*   **Loud Colors**:
    *   No neon, highly saturated primary colors, harsh contrasting gradients or aggressive reds/yellows.
*   **Clickbait Style**:
    *   No "Wait until the end!" or shocking arrows/emojis designed to manipulate attention.
    *   No fake urgency visual cues.
*   **Overdesigned Spirituality Clichés**:
    *   Avoid excessive lotus flowers, glowing third eyes, stereotypical "yoga studio" aesthetics, magical fairy dust, or generic mandalas unless deeply stylized and integrated minimally.
*   **Fake Luxury**:
    *   No generic gold foil textures.
    *   No cursive/script fonts that look like wedding invitations. (Elegance comes from typography structure and space, not just cursive letters.)
    *   No fake marble backgrounds.
*   **Over-corporate Educational Visuals**:
    *   No stock-photo business people pointing at charts.
    *   No cold, sterile geometric vector graphics commonly found in B2B SaaS marketing.
*   **Random Icon Usage**:
    *   Do not use standard flat UI icons unless they are fully customized to fit the brand's delicate aesthetic.
*   **Childish Wellness Aesthetics**:
    *   No blob shapes in bright pastels with funky bubbly fonts. The tone is deeply transformative, not a cute internet trend.
*   **Pushy Promotion**:
    *   Promotions must *invite*, not *coerce*. Do not use massive red "SALE" banners or aggressive strikethrough pricing visuals.
