# VISUAL IDENTITY RULES

This document defines the core visual intelligence, taste, and brand identity of Coach Sihame. The graphics designer brain must apply these rules to every visual decision.

## 1. Core Visual Taste

The design aesthetic must consistently exhibit these qualities:

*   **Premium**: High-quality, balanced, and sophisticated (not cheap or mass-produced).
*   **Calm**: Avoiding high contrast, visual noise, or chaotic layouts.
*   **Elegant**: Refined, graceful, and tasteful simplicity.
*   **Emotionally Safe**: creating an atmosphere of trust, containment, and warmth.
*   **Spiritually Grounded**: Evoking depth and presence without relying on clichés.
*   **Psychologically Intelligent**: Structuring information so it is easy to process and digest, matching the depth of the message.
*   **Minimal but not empty**: Using negative space intentionally, but ensuring the composition feels complete and deliberate.
*   **Soft but not weak**: Gentle visuals that still command respect and authority.
*   **Professional but never corporate**: Maintaining high standards without feeling sterile, transactional, or like a corporate "B2B" brand.

## 2. Visual Identity Preservation

When generating or structuring designs, the system must actively preserve the following elements:

*   **Identity Colors**: Adhere to Coach Sihame's core brand colors.
*   **Soft Premium Palette**: Utilize a primary palette of muted, earthy, or soft sophisticated tones. Avoid highly saturated primary colors.
*   **Arabic Readability**: The highest priority. Typography must never feel cramped. Arabic text requires proper line-spacing (leading), appropriate margins, and highly legible, premium font faces (e.g., Cairo, Tajawal, or similar elegant Arabic typefaces).
*   **Clean Spacing**: Ample breathing room around text boxes, logos, and focal elements.
*   **Clear Hierarchy**: The viewer's eye should naturally flow from the most important message to the secondary details.
*   **Non-Generic Composition**: Avoid standard "Canva templates". Each layout must feel bespoke to the meaning of the content.
*   **Subtle Emotional Atmosphere**: The background, lighting, and textures (if any) should communicate a subtle mood (e.g., morning reflection, quiet clarity, warm intimacy).
