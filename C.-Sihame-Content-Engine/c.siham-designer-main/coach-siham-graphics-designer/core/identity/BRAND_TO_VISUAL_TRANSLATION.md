# BRAND TO VISUAL TRANSLATION

## Purpose
Translate Coach Siham’s brand meaning into visual rules the designer brain can actually use.

This file is the bridge between:
- brand essence
- emotional territory
- symbolism
- composition
- typography
- prompt construction

---

## Core formula
**Brand meaning → emotional territory → visual mode → symbols → layout → prompt**

If this chain is broken, the output becomes generic.

---

## 1. Brand essence to visual consequence

### Brand essence
**العودة إلى السكون الذي يقود إلى الازدهار**

### Visual consequence
Designs should feel like:
- movement from tension to settling
- space opening after constriction
- inner order emerging gently
- growth as a quiet unfolding, not loud success imagery

### Never translate it as
- loud luxury
- loud optimism
- decorative wellness branding

---

## 2. Promise to visual consequence

### Brand promise
**الأمان قبل التغيير**

### Visual consequence
The design must prioritize:
- containment
- softness
- non-threatening clarity
- warm readability
- visual pacing

### For guided practice posts
The image must communicate:
- safety first
- then practice
- then stillness

Not just beauty.

## 5. Color meaning translation

> **Coach Confirmed:** Palette must balance **warmth** (terracotta, warm beige, orange tones) with **calm** (warm sage, greige). Never cold. Never a single monotone.

### Warm Cream / Linen `#F5EDE0`
Use for:
- primary background on all visuals
- breathing room, spaciousness, softness
- the base that makes all other colors feel warm

### Terracotta / Warm Sienna `#C4784A`
Use for:
- warmth, human containment, bodily safety
- accent frames, borders, warm visual anchors
- any design that needs to feel like "a warm embrace"
- **Prompt language:** "warm terracotta clay tones, muted burnt orange, earthy sienna"

### Warm Sage Green `#8A9E82`
Use for:
- healing, calm, fitrah, grounded growth
- must always lean warm, never blue-green or cool
- **Prompt language:** "muted warm sage green, earthy olive-tinted green, not teal"

### Champagne Gold `#C8A96B`
Use for:
- sacred refinement, divine light, calligraphic accents
- one glow point per design — never a background color
- **Prompt language:** "muted champagne gold, warm antique gold, not bright metallic"

### Warm Greige `#B5A898`
Use for:
- quiet neutral ground, secondary backgrounds
- mature stability, containment, silent depth
- **Prompt language:** "warm greige, warm taupe, soft parchment gray"

### Forbidden colors
- ❌ Deep teal / blue-green (too cold, feels clinical)
- ❌ Pure white (too sterile)
- ❌ Cool gray (disconnected, cold)
- ❌ Neon or bright accent colors

### Color pairing rule
- **Warmth layer:** Terracotta + Warm Cream → feels like a warm embrace
- **Calm layer:** Warm Sage + Warm Cream → feels like a healing space
- **Full balance:** Terracotta + Warm Sage + Warm Cream + Champagne Gold → the complete Coach Sihame palette
- Every design must use at least one warm color AND one calm color.

### Rule
No color should scream louder than the meaning.
Every color must feel like it belongs to a warm, safe, healing space.

## 6. Visual mode translation by post function

### Reflection post
Use:
- typography-first or atmospheric
- soft negative space
- low visual noise
- one reflective line if needed

### Guided practice post
Use:
- embodied/atmospheric mode
- title + selected support lines
- containment cue or subtle body cue
- safety must be visible

### Educational post
Use:
- symbolic/conceptual or educational mode
- strong hierarchy
- concept clarity over atmospheric softness

### Concept contrast post
Use:
- split composition
- paired symbolic logic
- instantly readable contrast

### Promo post
Use:
- title-led hierarchy
- clean CTA visibility
- still invitational, never loud

---

## 7. Text translation rules

### If the post is deep and long
Do not force full text on image.
Use:
- title or one reflective line
- rest stays in caption

### If the post is a guided practice
Use:
- one title that names the practice
- one or two lines that carry the practice meaning

### If the post is educational
Use:
- concept title
- short supporting breakdown if legible

---

## 8. Prompt translation formula
When prompting the image model, combine:

1. **Meaning cue**
2. **Mood cue**
3. **Visual mode**
4. **Symbol choice**
5. **Text-space requirement**
6. **Brand restraint rules**
7. **Avoid list**

### Example structure
- meaning: embodied reassurance
- mood: calm and safe
- mode: embodied/atmospheric
- symbol: subtle self-holding cue
- text-space: large upper-left negative space for Arabic
- brand restraint: premium, soft, non-generic
- avoid: stock-photo therapy clichés

---

## 9. Approval test
A translated visual direction is correct only if:
- the brand essence is still visible
- the post meaning is visible
- the visual mode is correct
- the Arabic text can live on it
- the output does not drift into generic calm aesthetic

If one of these fails, translation failed.
