# Benchmark Review Summary

## Purpose
Summarize what each benchmark is supposed to test before full generation/evaluation cycles.

---

## 01 — Reflection
**What success looks like:**
- paragraph-led or quiet reflective hierarchy
- embodied psychological meaning
- no generic calm-only aesthetic
- strong emotional safety

**Common failure risk:**
- pretty but semantically vague
- too much fabric / sphere / editorial softness with weak meaning anchor

---

## 02 — Guided Practice
**What success looks like:**
- clear safety / containment / self-contact cues
- text-first support with embodied reassurance
- visually regulating, not just soft

**Common failure risk:**
- calm mood without body meaning
- wellness cliché pose
- support image unrelated to the practice

---

## 03 — Educational
**What success looks like:**
- strong concept hierarchy
- obvious distinction between two psychological ideas
- text clarity over decoration

**Common failure risk:**
- elegant but under-explained
- weak contrast between concepts
- too much atmosphere, not enough teaching clarity

---

## 04 — Concept Contrast
**What success looks like:**
- visual opposition is immediate
- side-vs-side logic is readable
- contrast is conceptual, not cartoonish

**Common failure risk:**
- cluttered infographic look
- over-literal symbolism
- weak visual distinction between the two sides

---

## 05 — Promo
**What success looks like:**
- invitation without pressure
- elegant, clear, grounded
- offer feels premium and safe

**Common failure risk:**
- generic sales visual
- pushy CTA energy
- loss of brand softness under promo pressure


---

## 06 — PEAT Offer / Inner Maestro
**What success looks like:**
- educational/promo hybrid balance
- premium authority without pressure
- transformation from survival to flourishing is visually legible
- PEAT feels like a grounded path, not hype

**Common failure risk:**
- generic motivational poster energy
- promo tone overpowering psychological depth
- elegant mood without clear educational promise
