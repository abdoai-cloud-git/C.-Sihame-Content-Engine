# VISUAL TYPES

The visual decision engine must categorize each post into one of the following visual types, understanding the distinct layout, mood, and compositional approach required.

## 1. Quote Card
A short, deeply resonant statement.
*   **Text Hierarchy**: High focus on a central, short phrase. Minimal secondary text (just the author or handle).
*   **Layout Direction**: Centered, balanced, massive negative space.
*   **Mood**: Still, reflective, authoritative.

## 2. Educational Post
Teaching a concept, framework, or psychological/spiritual principle.
*   **Text Hierarchy**: Strong differentiation between headline (the hook/topic), sub-points, and body text.
*   **Layout Direction**: Structured, clear segmentation (bullet points, subtle dividers).
*   **Mood**: Clear, organized, grounded, highly readable.

## 3. Carousel Cover
The first slide of a multi-part post designed to generate curiosity and swipes.
*   **Text Hierarchy**: Bold, intriguing headline centered or left-aligned. Often features a visual anchor or subtle graphic indicating a sequence.
*   **Layout Direction**: High impact but clean. Do not overcrowd. Must leave room for a clear "swipe" indicator text/icon.
*   **Mood**: Inviting, curious, premium.

## 4. Story Visual (Instagram/Facebook)
Intimate, behind-the-scenes, or direct, fast communication.
*   **Text Hierarchy**: conversational, often relying on top/center placements to avoid overlapping UI elements.
*   **Layout Direction**: Vertical (9:16). Warm, human composition. Less rigid and structured than grid posts.
*   **Mood**: Human warmth, soft intimacy, spontaneous but tasteful.

## 5. Infographic-Style Post
Explaining relationships between ideas (e.g., boundaries vs. walls, nervous system states).
*   **Text Hierarchy**: Minimal explanatory text. Labels must be highly legible.
*   **Layout Direction**: Diagrammatic but elevated. Simple shapes (circles, soft rectangles, intersecting elements, Venns) with very soft borders.
*   **Mood**: Academic but emotionally intelligent and approachable.

## 6. Symbolic Emotional Visual
A post where the abstract concept (peace, inner child, containment) leads the design.
*   **Text Hierarchy**: Text is secondary or integrated seamlessly into a soft landscape or abstract texture.
*   **Layout Direction**: Open, fluid, less geometry, more organic shapes or premium photography.
*   **Mood**: Evocative, deeply spiritual, contemplative.

## 7. Promotion / Offer Visual
Announcing a masterclass, program, or retreat.
*   **Text Hierarchy**: Extremely high clarity. The offer name, dates, transformation outcome, and Call-to-Action (CTA) must be impossible to miss.
*   **Layout Direction**: Direct, central alignment or strong left-aligned structure. Less abstraction.
*   **Mood**: Invitational, confident, elegant. Never aggressive, pushy, or cluttered with "BUY NOW" urgency signs.

## 8. Reflection Visual
Longer-form thoughtful text, journaling prompts, or gentle inquiries.
*   **Text Hierarchy**: Paragraph structure. Must prioritize line-height (leading) for readability of longer Arabic text.
*   **Layout Direction**: Clean, editorial-style layout. Looks like a page from a beautiful, minimal book or journal.
*   **Mood**: Quiet, paced, intellectually and emotionally safe.
